Stocks Waver, Look Past Manufacturing Jump Stocks Waver, Look Past Manufacturing Jump 1 hours                                                                             
											                                                                          Stocks fluctuated on Tuesday, with the S&amp;P 500 on the decline after hitting another intraday record, as investors largely looked beyond data showing that manufacturing jumped to its highest level in three years.The Dow Jones Industrial Average rebounded after sliding early, but then fell again and was 47 points down in early afternoon trading. The S&amp;P 500 fell 4 points but the Nasdaq added 5 points. The Institute for Supply Management reported the pace of growth in the U.S. manufacturing sector rose in August to its highest level since March 2011. The ISM index rose to 59, from July's 57.1. &quot;It's great news but not new news,&quot; said Art Hogan, chief market strategist at Wunderlich Securities. &quot;The ISM is a great reading and is a confirmation of what the market has done,&quot; said Hogan, referring to Wall Street's advance, which has the S&amp;P 500 hitting record highs and up 8.5 percent this year. Separately, the Commerce Department reported construction spending rose 1.8 percent in July. Financial-data firm Markit reported its final measure of U.S. manufacturing in August came in at 57.9, down from an initial read of 58.
