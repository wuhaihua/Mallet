Mass escape from Nashville youth detention center Mass escape from Nashville youth detention center Thirty-two teens escaped from a Nashville youth detention center late Monday night. Most were still on the loose early Tuesday.Authorities at the Woodland Hills Youth Detention Center say the breakout happened during a shift change, with the escapees, all 14 to 17 years old, managing to climb under a fence.Of the 32 who got out, only six had been apprehended by early Tuesday, reports CBS Nashville affiliate WTVF-TV. They were caught near the facility. An intense search was underway for the rest.There's been no criminal activity was reported in the area since the escape, the station says.There were roughly 78 teens at the detention center in all.
