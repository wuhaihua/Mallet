The US Embassy in Tripoli is being safeguarded and has not been ransacked after Islamist militias moved into an annex of the complex, America's ambassador to Libya said today.                                                                    
					                                                                          A senior State Department official said Sunday that the U.S. Embassy in Libya's capital is believed to be secure after reports that an Islamist-allied militia group took over the compound.&quot;We've seen the reports and videos and are seeking additional details,&quot; said the official. &quot;At this point, we believe the Embassy compound itself remains secure but we continue to monitor the situation on the ground, which remains very fluid.&quot;The U.S. embassy personnel and operations in Tripoli were relocated on July 26th due to the ongoing fighting between militias occurring very close to the compound.&quot;We are committed to continuing to support the Libyan people, to work with the Libyan government, and to return our people to Tripoli as soon as the security situation allow,&quot; said Secretary of State John Kerry in August.
