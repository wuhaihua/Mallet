Oversold Conditions For Laurentian Bank of Canada (LB) Oversold Conditions For Laurentian Bank of Canada (LB) Legendary investor Warren Buffett advises to be fearful when others are greedy, and be greedy when others are fearful. One way we can try to measure the level of fear in a given stock is through a technical analysis indicator called the Relative Strength Index, or RSI, which measures momentum on a scale of zero to 100. A stock is considered to be oversold if the RSI reading falls below 30. 
In trading on Tuesday, shares of Laurentian Bank of Canada (Toronto: LB) entered into oversold territory, hitting an RSI reading of 28.9, after changing hands as low as $49.06 per share. By comparison, the current RSI reading of the S&amp;P/TSX Composite Index is 66.1. A bullish investor could look at LB’s 28.9 RSI reading today as a sign that the recent heavy selling is in the process of exhausting itself, and begin to look for entry point opportunities on the buy side.
